# Stance Analysis Summary

The stance analysis is based only on content with explicit positive or negative positioning. Neutral or purely descriptive posts are excluded from this table.

The results show an overwhelmingly negative stance toward Iran, with 99.90 percent of explicit content classified as negative.

The United States is evaluated more ambivalently. While 71 percent of explicit content is negative, 29 percent is positive, reflecting its dual role as both a security provider and a source of perceived risk.

The Israeli government is evaluated entirely negatively in explicit stance content, though it is not the primary narrative focus of the dataset.

Public attitudes toward the continuation of war and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz are strongly negative, indicating a dominant preference for de-escalation and regional stability.

US military bases in Kuwait show the most balanced distribution by content count, but this issue requires separate engagement-weighted analysis because opposition may carry greater visibility.
