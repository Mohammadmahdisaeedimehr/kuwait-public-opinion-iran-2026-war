# Sensitivity Matrix Summary

The sensitivity matrix evaluates each major narrative dimension based on three factors:

- Narrative weight
- Public sensitivity
- Potential for de-escalation

The dominant finding is that Iran-related narratives form the core of the threat perception structure. This axis combines the highest narrative weight with the highest sensitivity and low de-escalation potential, making it structurally difficult to shift.

US military bases represent a secondary but highly sensitive issue. The debate is driven less by ideology and more by perceived effectiveness and security trade-offs.

Identity and internal betrayal narratives have relatively lower volume but very low de-escalation potential. These narratives introduce social fragmentation and are difficult to neutralize once activated.

In contrast, the Persian Gulf (خلیج فارس), energy security, and missile defense topics show lower sensitivity but higher potential for de-escalation. These areas are more technical and less identity-driven, making them suitable entry points for tension reduction.

Diplomacy and ceasefire narratives, although marginal in volume, show the highest potential for de-escalation. Their expansion could play a key role in reshaping the overall narrative environment.
